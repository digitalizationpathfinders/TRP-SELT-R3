Before you begin

The CRA may grant relief of penalties and/or interest charges when an individual or business experiences circumstances beyond their control that prevent them from meeting their tax obligations.

Penalties and interest are generally charged when you file or pay late. Check your Notice of Assessment (NOA) or notice of reassessment to verify what type of charges you have.

Use this tool to find out if your situation may qualify, and help prepare you to fill out your relief request.

info
You can request relief of interest for any year, but relief can only be granted for interest you accumulated within the last 10 years.
For example, if a request for relief was submitted in 2024, and the interest began accumulating in 2010, relief may only be granted for the interest that accumulated between 2014 to 2024.

Third-party error - This situation may not qualify for relief under the Taxpayer Relief Program
You are responsible for any errors or delays caused by a third-party (for example, your accountant). You are expected to know what was filed and get proof that your return or payments were sent on time. However, there may be exceptional situations where relief can be granted.
info
Bank error - This situation may not qualify for relief under the Taxpayer Relief Program
A bank error may qualify for taxpayer relief if a payment is made on time at a financial institution, but a delay occurs in the transfer of funds to the CRA. These types of requests may be approved if the bank has admitted fault and can provide proof of the error.
